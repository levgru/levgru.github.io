Research

  • Quantum Optics Group, Harvey Mudd College Spring 2024 - Present +

    The HMC Quantum Optics group (or Lynn Lab), led by Professor Theresa Lynn, investigates quantum communication protocols for polarization-entangled two photon systems. Our current work focuses on developing a two-step adaptive choice method for determining the entanglement of two photons via a linear optical apparatus.
    Check back Spring 2025 for an update on my theory work for the group, and see Professor Lynn's page for a more general overview of the group's work: https://www.hmc.edu/physics/faculty-staff/lynn/.

    Quantum Materials and Nanodevice Laboratory, San Francisco State University Summer 2023 +

    The Quantum Materials Lab was led by the late Professor AKM Newaz at SFSU. As an undergraduate researcher, I studied shifts in the Raman peaks of Molybdenum Disulfide when developed on gold substrate in ambient vs. glove box environments. Using Raman spectrometry and Python for data analysis, we confirmed differences in biaxial strain and doping between the two preparation situations, showing potential impacts for solar and biomedical applications.
