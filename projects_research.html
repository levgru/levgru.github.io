01. Research and Projects

01a.

Research

  • Quantum Optics Group, Harvey Mudd College Spring 2024 - Present +

    The Harvey Mudd Quantum Optics group studies entanglement witnesses and exchange-symmetry in quantum computing. I contributed to experimental demonstrations of a two-step process for detecting entanglement in unknown two-photon states, as well as data analysis and optimization routines. See Professor Lynn's page for an overview, and my home page for a summary and poster PDF.

  • Quantum Materials and Nanodevice Laboratory, SFSU Summer 2023 +

    The Quantum Materials and Nanodevice Lab investigates properties of two-dimensional materials and their applications toward nanodevices. I worked on Raman spectrometry and data analysis of monolayer MoS2 on various substrates. Unique Raman peaks revealed new material insights.

01b.

Projects

  • Modeling Bio-Materials to Second Order Fall 2024 +

    We modeled biological materials with a second-order correction to Hooke's law and simulated dynamic systems of non-linear springs. See paper and code for results.

    Read the Write Up View Code on GitHub
  • Demonstrating Ammonia SDMP via Variational Quantum Eigensolvers, Spring 2024 +

    I calculated ground state energies for ammonia and hydrogen using VQE quantum algorithms, confirming symmetric dual minima potential (SDMP) predictions. All code and figures are open source.

    Read the Write Up View Code on GitHub
  • A Physical and Chemical Simulation for Estimating Long-Term Stair Wear (COMAP MCM 2025), Jan 2025 +

    We built a simulation using a 2D Generalized Maxwell Model and Gaussian Mixture Model to model stair wear and chemical erosion over time for the 2025 COMAP contest. Full paper/code available.
